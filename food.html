Quincy Market

Quincy Market is a great place to hang around with friends and family and enjoy some good food. There are 18 restaurants and 35 colonnade eateries of all cuisines. You will surely find something that you like. Many people ask what’s the best Bostonian food or what is the must-eat in Quincy Market. Classic favorites include:

  • Clam Chowder
  • Boston cream pie - official dessert of Massachusetts
  • Bostonian baked beans with brown bread
  • Lobster rolls
  • Seafood (lobster, whole belly clams)

Eataly

Eataly is the largest Italian marketplace in the world, committed to promoting high-quality yet affordable regional Italian food with a local twist. As of now, Eataly USA comprises Eataly New York Flatiron, Eataly Chicago, Eataly NYC Downtown, Eataly Boston, Eataly L.A., and Eataly Las Vegas – all modeled after the first Eataly in Turin, Italy, founded by Oscar Farinetti in 2007.

Eataly Boston is a three-floor authentic Italian experience with plenty for guests to eat, shop and learn including: four full-service restaurants; retail counters (including meat, seafood, fresh pasta, pastry and chocolate, cheese and cured meats); two coffee bars; a bakery; a salad bar; a mozzarella lab; a gelato counter; every essential grocery department including sweets, spreads and jams, hundreds of olive oils, and dry pastas (sourced from both Italy and New England); a housewares section; a book store; a cooking school; various private event spaces, and much more!

Boston Public Market

The Boston Public Market is an indoor, year-round marketplace featuring about 30 New England artisans and food producers housed under one roof offering fresh foods, prepared meals, crafts, and specialty items. Residents and visitors alike can find seasonal, locally sourced food from Massachusetts and New England, including fresh produce, meat and poultry, eggs, dairy, seafood, baked goods, specialty items, crafts, and prepared breakfast, lunch, and dinner options. Everything sold at the Market is produced or originates in New England, as the seasons allow.
